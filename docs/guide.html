How to Display Lyrics and Timers on Google Meet

A complete guide to adding text overlays, countdown timers, and worship lyrics to your Google Meet video calls.

5 min read Last updated: January 2026

Whether you're leading a virtual worship service, teaching an online class, or running a remote meeting, displaying text overlays and timers directly on your camera feed can make your video calls more engaging and professional.

This guide shows you how to use Meet Camera Overlay, a free Chrome extension, to add lyrics, announcements, countdown timers, and custom text to your Google Meet camera.

What You Can Do

With Meet Camera Overlay, you can:

  • Display worship lyrics during virtual church services
  • Show countdown timers for tests, activities, or sermon segments
  • Add announcements like service times, giving links, or daily agendas
  • Show custom text for any purpose - lesson objectives, scripture verses, instructions
  • Add image and GIF overlays for branding or visual effects

Step-by-Step Setup

1 Install the Extension

Visit the Chrome Web Store and search for "Meet Camera Overlay" or click the install button on our homepage. Click "Add to Chrome" and confirm the installation. The extension icon will appear in your Chrome toolbar.

2 Open the Settings Panel

Click the Meet Camera Overlay icon in your Chrome toolbar. This opens the settings panel where you can create and manage your overlays.

3 Create a Text Overlay

Click "Add Text Banner" to create a new text overlay. Enter your text (lyrics, announcement, or message), then customize the font size, color, background, and position. The "lower third" position works well for lyrics and announcements.

4 Set Up a Timer (Optional)

Click "Add Timer" to create a countdown or count-up timer. Choose your duration, select a corner position, and customize the appearance. Timers are perfect for timed activities, sermon segments, or test periods.

5 Join Google Meet

Start or join a Google Meet call. Your overlays will automatically appear on your camera feed. Use the extension popup to toggle overlays on/off, edit text, or start/stop timers during the call.

Pro Tip You can prepare multiple text overlays in advance and quickly switch between them during your call. This is especially useful for worship services where you need to display different song lyrics.

Use Cases

For Churches

Worship Lyrics Display

Show song lyrics on the worship leader's or pastor's camera feed during virtual services. Remote attendees can follow along without needing a separate screen share.

Scripture and Responsive Readings

Display Bible verses during sermons or responsive readings. Participants can read along directly from the video feed.

Service Information

Show giving links, service times, event announcements, or welcome messages to virtual attendees.

For Educators

Activity Timers

Display countdown timers during tests, group work, reading time, or transitions. Students always know exactly how much time remains.

Lesson Information

Show today's topic, learning objectives, homework assignments, or instructions without sharing your entire screen.

Daily Schedules

Display the day's schedule, lunch menu, or announcements during virtual homeroom sessions.

Tips for Best Results

  • Keep text concise - Shorter text is easier to read on video. Break long content into multiple overlays.
  • Use high contrast colors - Light text on dark backgrounds (or vice versa) is most readable.
  • Position thoughtfully - Lower third overlays don't block faces. Corner timers stay out of the way.
  • Test before going live - Preview your overlays before important calls to ensure they look right.
  • Prepare in advance - Set up all your overlays before the call so you can focus on presenting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this work with Zoom or Microsoft Teams?

Currently, Meet Camera Overlay is designed specifically for Google Meet. Support for other platforms may be added in future updates.

Can attendees see my overlays?

Yes! The overlays are added directly to your camera feed, so everyone in the meeting sees them on your video.

Does it work with virtual backgrounds?

Yes, Meet Camera Overlay works alongside Google Meet's virtual backgrounds and other camera effects.

Is there a cost?

No, Meet Camera Overlay is completely free and open source.
